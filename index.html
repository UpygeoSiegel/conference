Sergio Arteaga

I was born and raised in Inglewood, California, the son of immigrant parents from Mexico. I was a founding student of Green Dot Public Schools, University Prep's original founders. Though not always an easy road, I persevered through high school and attended college in Los Angeles at Occidental College and earned a degree in International Relations. In 2008 I made the decision to move to New York City to pursue a Masters degree at Teachers College and to help establish University Prep Charter High School (formerly Green Dot New York Charter High School) as a founding faculty member. I have been incredibly fortunate enough to be married to an amazing educator, business owner and professor and I am the proud father of 2 young daughters. They are without a doubt my reason for trying to bring my best everyday. Education has always been a part of my life even when I didn't realize it. My goal is to not just provide lessons within the classroom but for my students to walk away with life lessons that will benefit them once they leave University Prep.

Nathaniel Bouie

Nathaniel Bouie is native of Rochester, NY and joins the UPrep family after eight years of teaching. He relocated to the NYC area in 2014 to expand his career and educational interests. In doing so, he taught a variety of subjects and coached varsity basketball, demonstrating an ability to lead among several schools in the northern sector of the Bronx. Beyond education, his interests include learning, fitness and team sports of all kinds. Mr. Bouie began his post secondary studies at Monroe Community College (Rochester, NY) and worked tirelessly to earn graduate honors. He would then move on to become a proud graduate of SUNY Cortland (Cortland, NY) and Mercy College (Bronx, NY) where he earned his undergraduate and graduate degrees. Mr. Bouie is currently a doctoral candidate at Pepperdine University (Malibu, CA). His commitment to urban education is driven by his own experiences and inspirations. Mr. Bouie uses his own life as evidence to support the idea that students of color from Impoverished urban communities can achieve. It is his unwavering belief in this idea that has allowed him to build meaningful relationships with students, parents and colleagues. When an opportunity was presented, Mr. Bouie excitedly joined the UPrep administration in 2020 as an Assistant Principal.

Dumitru Grigorean

Dumitru David Grigorean, or Mr G. as he’s fondly known to his students, is an energetic, proud, and enthusiastic University Prep Charter High School teacher. He was born and raised in Romania by his maternal grandparents. In 2007, when he was 14 years old, he moved to New York City. Adaptation to his new surroundings was tough. He did not speak the language, did not have any friends here, and was not familiar with American culture. He had to learn everything anew. He started his studies, at the time, classified as an English Language Learner, at Grover Cleveland High School in Ridgewood, Queens. He was blessed to come across some incredibly dedicated educators who offered their free time to work with him. With their help, he graduated as Salutatorian of his class. He continued his studies at Queens College, from which he graduated with a degree in Secondary Education and History, Summa Cum Laude. It was also at Queens College that Mr. G earned his master’s degree in history. In 2015, Mr. Grigorean joined Teach for America and was assigned to University Prep Charter High School where he began his teaching career. He is still there to this day. Mr. Grigorean is very passionate about teaching and is also an avid soccer fan. His favorite team is Chelsea F.C.

Pedro Noguera

Pedro Noguera is one of the nation’s leading scholars on issues related to racial inequality and policy aimed at improving education for all. Prior to coming to USC to serve as the Emery Stoops and Joyce King Stoops Dean of the Rossier School of Education, he served as a Professor of Education and holder of endowed chairs at UCLA, NYU, Harvard University and the University of California, Berkeley. He is the author of 15 books. His most recent book A Search for Common Ground: Conversations About the Tough Questions and Complex Issues Confronting K-12 Education in the United States Today with Rick Hess was the winner of the American Association of Publishers Prose Award in 2021). Noguera has served as an advisor to several states, school districts, foundations and nonprofits. In 2022 he was appointed to the President’s National Commission on Equity and Excellence in Education for Hispanics. In 2014 he was elected to the National Academy of Education and Phi Delta Kappa honor society, and in 2020 Noguera was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. Noguera has received eight honorary doctorates from American universities, and received awards from the Center for the Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University, the National Association of Secondary School Principals, and from the McSilver Institute for Poverty Policy and Research at NYU for his research and advocacy efforts aimed at fighting poverty. In 2023 he was ranked 1st in the nation for influence and impact in the field of education, by Education Week.
